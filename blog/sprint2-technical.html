What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

A technical post by Cam.

When you first start to play around with CSS you will come to understand that everything (text, images, all content) has a box around it. You may not see this box at first, but you will soon praise this box.

This ‘box’ is made up of a few different things…

1. The Content

The content is whatever it is you inserted in your HTML. It could be some text saying “Hello world” or maybe an image of a gazebo. This content can be sized with the width and height properties.

2. Padding

The padding is what sits around the content and ‘lets it breathe’. It's an invisible space that is controlled by the padding property in CSS.

3. Border

The border is exactly what you think it is, it’s a border that sits around the content AND the padding. Just like padding it can be controlled (sized, styled) by using the border property in CSS.

4. Margin

The margin is outside everything else we have mentioned. It sits after the border. It wraps together the content, padding, and border in one box and separates it from another piece of content or another ‘element’ as we call it in HTML. Just like the padding it's also invisible (whitespace), and it can be controlled using the margin property in CSS.
